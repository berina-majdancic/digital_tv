How Digital TV Works

Digital TV works by converting audio and video signals into binary code—sequences of 0s and 1s—that can be compressed, transmitted efficiently, and decoded by receivers.

1. Compression

Digital signals are compressed using codecs such as MPEG-2, MPEG-4, or H.264. Compression reduces the file size while maintaining high quality, allowing more content to be transmitted.

2. Transmission

Compressed data is transmitted over various media—such as terrestrial signals, cable, satellite, or IP networks. The type of transmission affects the quality and latency of the service.

3. Reception and Decoding

A digital tuner or set-top box receives the broadcast signal and decodes the compressed data, converting it into video and audio that your TV can display.

4. Display

The decoded signal is shown on the screen in SD, HD, or 4K resolution, depending on the source and display capabilities. Modern TVs also process color and motion for smoother output.

5. Error Correction

Digital TV systems use error correction algorithms to detect and fix transmission errors, ensuring consistent quality even in low-signal conditions.
