Digital TV Standards

Various regions use different technical standards for broadcasting digital television. These standards define how data is encoded, transmitted, and received.

1. ATSC (Advanced Television Systems Committee)

Used mainly in North America, ATSC supports both standard-definition and high-definition digital broadcasts. The newer ATSC 3.0 standard adds internet-like capabilities.

2. DVB (Digital Video Broadcasting)

Common in Europe, DVB is a family of standards (DVB-T for terrestrial, DVB-S for satellite, DVB-C for cable). It's widely adopted and supports HD and UHD formats.

3. ISDB (Integrated Services Digital Broadcasting)

Developed in Japan, ISDB allows for mobile reception and emergency broadcasting. It's used in Japan and many Latin American countries.
