What is Digital TV?

Digital TV (DTV) transmits audio and video by digitally processed and multiplexed signals, in contrast to the older analog TV which used analog signals. This allows for higher quality picture and sound, more channels, and interactive features.

Examples include High Definition (HDTV) and Ultra High Definition (4K) broadcasts, which offer superior picture and sound quality compared to analog signals. Smart TVs with built-in streaming apps like Netflix, Hulu, and Disney+ are also part of the digital TV ecosystem, allowing users to access on-demand content via the internet. Additionally, digital cable and satellite services such as Xfinity, DirecTV, and Dish Network provide hundreds of channels with digital clarity and interactive features like video-on-demand and digital program guides.
