Types of Digital Television

Digital TV can be delivered through various platforms, each using different technologies and infrastructure. The main types include:

1. Terrestrial Digital TV (DTT)

Also known as over-the-air digital TV, this type is broadcast using traditional terrestrial signals, but in digital format. It requires a digital antenna and is often free to access.

2. Satellite Digital TV

Delivered via satellites orbiting the Earth, this form of digital TV offers wide coverage, especially in rural or remote areas. It typically requires a satellite dish and receiver.

3. Cable Digital TV

Provided through coaxial or fiber optic cable networks, cable TV often includes bundled services such as internet and phone. It is subscription-based and commonly found in urban areas.

5. OTT (Over-the-Top) Streaming

OTT platforms deliver content via the public internet, bypassing traditional broadcasters. Services like Netflix, YouTube, and Hulu fall under this category and are accessible on smart TVs, phones, or PCs.

6. Hybrid Models

Many modern setups combine traditional broadcast TV with internet features, such as HbbTV in Europe, which allows users to access additional information and streaming services alongside live channels.
